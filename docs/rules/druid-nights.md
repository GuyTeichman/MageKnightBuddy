# Druid Nights

Source: `Mage-Knight-Board-Game-Ultimate-Edition-Rule-Book-September-2018.pdf` (base rulebook, Scenario Book), page 21.

**Competitive only** — the rulebook prints no Solo or Cooperative variant for this scenario, unlike e.g. First Reconnaissance or the Apocalypse Dragon scenarios. Not a v1 target (v1 is Solo Conquest only) — captured here as reference for when this scenario gets implemented (whether as-is for multiplayer, or house-ruled into a solo variant).

## Overview

- Players: 2 to 4. Type: Slightly competitive, or competitive.
- Length: Four rounds (2 days and 2 nights).
- Purpose: a shorter scenario similar to the standard one, but with different goals — mysterious obelisks with engraved symbols have appeared on the magical glades around this kingdom, and players are sent to investigate.

## Setup

- Map Shape: Wedge (2 or 3 players), or Open limited to 4 columns (4 players).
- Countryside tiles: 8, 9, or 11 (remove only tiles with no magical glades on them).
- Core city tiles: 1 (always the Green city).
- Core non-city tiles: 1, 2, or 3 (one less than the number of players).
- Cities: the city is friendly. Each player puts one Shield token on it, but no-one is its leader.

## Special rules

- **Activating a magical glade**: ending your movement there lets you mark it with your Shield token. Multiple tokens can be on each glade, but only one from each player.
- **Incantation**: once during the first Night, on any of your turns, you may perform an incantation. You may not be at an inhabited space, and it counts as your Action for that turn. It summons one monster for each glade you have your Shield token on — draw that many brown enemies and fight them.
  - Enemies you defeat bring you twice as much Fame.
  - Enemies you don't defeat disappear after the combat (but any Wounds they dealt do not).
  - Regardless of how many you defeated, at the end of your turn you get one random crystal for each monster summoned (roll a die for each, as in a monster den).
- At the end of the first Night, remove all Shield tokens from the glades. Players have to mark them again if they want to use them for an incantation the second Night.
- During the second Night, you may do the same, except this time you summon red enemies. The Fame for defeated ones is doubled again, and this time you get two random crystals for each enemy.

## Scenario end

When all players have performed the incantation during the second Night (in that case, each player gets one more turn), or at the end of the second Night.

## Scoring

Apply standard Achievements scoring. Whoever has the most Fame wins the game.

## Variants

- **More competitive**: only one (with 2 players) or two (with 3 or 4 players) Shield tokens can be placed on each glade. If a glade is already at its limit, choose and replace one from another player.
- **Epic** (with fewer players): 3 days and 3 nights. Use all Countryside and Core non-city tiles. Remove the Shields after the second Night too. During the third Night, summon one brown and one red enemy for each magical glade you have marked, and get three crystals for each pair summoned.

## Outcome (Won/Lost)

The rulebook frames this as a Fame race with no separate win/lose condition — whoever has the most Fame at scenario end wins (see Scoring). There's no additional pass/fail condition layered on top, unlike Mines Liberation's or Dungeon Lords' "liberated/conquered everything" check.
