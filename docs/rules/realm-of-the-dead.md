# The Realm of the Dead

Source: `Mage-Knight-Board-Game-Ultimate-Edition-Expansion-Rule-Books-September-2018.pdf` (Shades of Tezla expansion, Scenario Book), pages 24–25.

Not a v1 target (v1 is Solo Conquest only) — captured here as reference for when this scenario gets implemented.

## Overview

- Length: Four Rounds (2 days and 2 nights).
- Purpose: defeat the Necromancer Lord and cleanse the Realm of the Dead. A Necromancer has arisen in a province abandoned since the Breaking, raising legions of the fallen while working to complete the Ritual of Lichdom.

### Special rules (apply to all variants)

- **Enemy tokens**: all Dark Crusader enemy tokens are sorted into separate piles. Rampaging enemies are picked from the Dark Crusader pile. If a rampaging Dark Crusader token was placed because a player explored (rather than at game setup), that player puts one of their Shield tokens on it — from the following turn, that enemy token gains Pursuit with regard to that player only (it still rampages normally with regard to everyone else).
- **Magical Glades**: revealed on a countryside tile → a Graveyard tile is placed over it, with enemy token(s) placed face down according to the order the Glade was placed: 1st → two green Dark Crusader tokens; 2nd → one green + one brown Dark Crusader token; 3rd → one red Dark Crusader token; 4th → one green + one red Dark Crusader token; 5th → one brown + one red Dark Crusader token.
- **Liberate Location**: Night rules apply on a Graveyard, but none of its other effects work until it's liberated. To liberate it, enter its space as an Action and fight all enemies there — defeating only some leaves the rest, attackable again later. Defeating the last enemy liberates the Graveyard, granting a reward by the order it was placed: 1st → an Advanced Action, 2nd → a Spell, 3rd → a Unit (per the normal rules for gaining a Unit from a Ruins token), 4th → an Artifact, 5th → an Artifact **and** an Advanced Action. Once liberated, any player on that location may pay 1 mana (not an Action) to seal it, preventing the contamination from returning — they mark it with a Shield token and gain +1 Reputation. Each Graveyard can only be sealed once, and not while another player's Hero occupies it.
- **Cities**: when the Blue City tile is placed, place the Necropolis location tile over the city space, with the Dark Crusader's enemy tokens face up per the faction leader's level. You must move into the Necropolis space to attack the Necromancer and his followers — it is not a safe space.
- **Additional special rules**: start with 1 Fame (instead of 0); +1 extra Fame whenever you cross a line on the Fame track. Start with +2 Reputation (a bonus of +1 for interaction, from the start). One extra die in the Source, and one extra Unit in the offer.

## Competitive

- Players: 2 to 4. Type: Competitive.
- Map Shape: Wedge (2 or 3 players), or Open limited to 4 columns (4 players).
- Countryside tiles: 5, 7, or 9 (exactly 2, 3, or 4 should contain a Magical Glade).
- Core city tiles: 2 — the Blue City tile and the Green City tile (Green City is friendly: each player puts one Shield token on it, no-one is leader).
- Core non-city tiles: 2, 3, or 4 (equal to the number of players).
- Dark Crusader leader tile: represents the Necromancer, starting level = number of players + 3.

**Scenario end**: all Graveyards sealed and the Necromancer defeated → all players get one last turn (game ends immediately if the Round ends during this).

**Scoring**: apply standard Achievements Scoring. In addition, count each player's Shield tokens on a Graveyard or on the Dark Crusader leader token — the most gets +5 Fame ("Greatest Enemy" of that faction). Ties broken in favor of whoever's Shield token sits on the highest level of the faction leader token.

## Cooperative

- Players: 2 to 4. Type: Cooperative.
- Map Shape: Fully Open.
- Countryside tiles: 6, 8, or 10 (exactly 3, 4, or 5 should contain a Magical Glade).
- Core city tiles: 2, same as Competitive (Blue City, Green City friendly).
- Core non-city tiles: 2, 3, or 4 (equal to the number of players). Dark Crusader leader tile starts at level = 2 × number of players + 4.
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player.
- Cards/Skills: remove the four competitive Spells (109–112); use the Lost Legion expansion's cooperative interactive Skill tokens instead of competitive ones; remove competitive Skills from every player's Skill deck, including the Dummy Player's.
- Additional rules: Dummy Player takes a random Tactic first, then the real players choose theirs. Team Rules apply, except everyone is one team.

**Scenario end**: all Graveyards sealed and the Necromancer defeated → all players (except the Dummy Player) get one last turn (game ends immediately if the Round ends during this).

**Scoring**: one team score. Base = the lowest Fame among all players. Apply standard Achievements Scoring, except each category only scores the single player with the highest value (most negative for Greatest Beating) — no Titles. Then:
- +5 points for each Graveyard sealed, +10 points for defeating the Necromancer. +10 more points if you sealed all of the Graveyards *and* also defeated the Necromancer.
- +20 points if every player has at least one Shield token on the faction leader token.
- +30 points for each Round finished before the Round limit.
- +1 point for each card remaining in the Dummy Player's Deed deck not yet flipped this Round.
- +5 points if "End of the Round" was not yet announced this Round.

## Solo

- Players: 1. Type: Solo.
- Map Shape: Wedge.
- Countryside tiles: 5 (exactly 2 should contain a Magical Glade).
- Core city tiles: 2 — the Blue City tile and the Green City tile (both the player and the Dummy Player put a Shield token on it, no-one is leader).
- Core non-city tiles: 1. Dark Crusader leader tile starts at level 4.
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player.
- Cards/Skills: same as Cooperative (remove Spells 109–112; use Lost Legion cooperative interactive Skill tokens; remove competitive Skills from both decks).
- Additional rules: the player always chooses Tactics first, the Dummy Player takes one random card from those remaining. At the end of each Day/Night, remove both used Tactic cards from the game.

**Scenario end**: both Graveyards sealed and the Necromancer defeated → the player has one last turn (the Dummy Player does not).

**Scoring**: base = your Fame. Apply standard Achievements Scoring, no Titles. Then:
- +5 points for each Graveyard sealed, +10 points for defeating the Necromancer. +10 more points if you sealed both Graveyards *and* also defeated the Necromancer.
- +30 points for each Round finished before the Round limit.
- +1 point for each card remaining in the Dummy Player's Deed deck not yet flipped this Round.
- +5 points if "End of the Round" was not yet announced this Round.

## Outcome (Won/Lost) — all variants

Won iff every placed Graveyard was sealed *and* the Necromancer was defeated; Lost otherwise. A score is always counted either way — the rulebook is explicit that failing still lets you "count your score to see how good you were."
