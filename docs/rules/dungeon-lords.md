# Dungeon Lords

Source: `Mage-Knight-Board-Game-Ultimate-Edition-Rule-Book-September-2018.pdf` (base rulebook, Scenario Book), page 21.

**Competitive only** — the rulebook prints no Solo or Cooperative variant for this scenario, unlike e.g. First Reconnaissance or the Apocalypse Dragon scenarios. Not a v1 target (v1 is Solo Conquest only) — captured here as reference for when this scenario gets implemented (whether as-is for multiplayer, or house-ruled into a solo variant).

## Overview

- Players: 2 to 4. Type: Competitive.
- Length: Five rounds (3 days and 2 nights).
- Purpose: a shorter scenario similar to the standard one, but with different goals — a vast system of underground tunnels was discovered beneath the kingdom, dug by persons unknown, and players are sent to seize control of them.

## Setup

- Map Shape: Wedge (2 or 3 players), or Open limited to 4 columns (4 players).
- Countryside tiles: 8, 9, or 11 (remove only tiles with no dungeons on them).
- Core city tiles: 1 (always the Blue city).
- Core non-city tiles: 1, 2, or 3 (always include the desert tile with the monastery and tomb).
- Cities: the city is friendly. Each player puts one Shield token on it, but no-one is its leader.

## Special rules

- **Secret dungeon entrances**: whenever a tile with a village is revealed, the player who revealed it chooses any accessible non-swamp space adjacent to that village (even on a neighboring tile) that has no site on it. From now on, there's a secret dungeon entrance on that space. Put a face-down brown enemy token there to mark it.
  - A secret dungeon entrance works like any other dungeon (see its description card). Conquering it marks the space and claims the reward. Failing to conquer it discards the monster token and puts another random brown enemy on that tile.
- **Secret tombs**: the same applies for monasteries, except a red enemy token is put on an adjacent empty space to mark a secret tomb. A secret tomb works the same way as a regular tomb.
- Conquered dungeons and tombs (both regular and secret) cannot be entered again for more Fame in this scenario.
- **Underground travel**: all conquered dungeons and tombs (including secret ones) are connected.
  - When on a conquered dungeon or tomb space during movement, you may move directly to any other conquered dungeon or tomb space.
  - The two spaces don't have to be the same type (e.g. a regular dungeon to a hidden tomb).
  - Cost: 2 Move points, plus 1 Move point for each space traveled (measuring the distance between the two spaces).
  - When measuring that distance, you have to go through revealed spaces, and you have to avoid lakes and swamps. If that's not possible, you cannot travel there this way.

## Scenario end

When all map tiles are revealed and all dungeons and tombs are conquered, all players (including whoever conquered the last one) have one last turn. If the Round ends during this, the game ends immediately.

## Scoring

If you conquered all the dungeons and tombs, the mission was successful. In any case, apply standard Achievements scoring, except:

- When scoring Adventures (Greatest Adventurer), also count the secret dungeons and tombs. All dungeons and tombs give 4 points instead of 2.
- Instead of the Greatest Adventurer title, the Greatest Dungeon Crawler title is awarded, worth +5 points (+2 if shared/tied).

## Outcome (Won/Lost)

Won iff all dungeons and tombs were conquered; Lost otherwise. A score is always computed either way.
