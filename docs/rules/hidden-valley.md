# The Hidden Valley

Source: `Mage-Knight-Board-Game-Ultimate-Edition-Expansion-Rule-Books-September-2018.pdf` (Shades of Tezla expansion, Scenario Book), pages 25–26.

## Overview

- Length: Four Rounds (2 days and 2 nights).
- Purpose: find the Hidden Valley and defeat the High Priestess. Rumors tell of an Elementalist High Priestess gathering forces in a valley so well hidden even the Council of the Void can't find it.

### Special rules (apply to all variants)

- **Enemy tokens**: all Elementalist enemy tokens are sorted into separate piles. Rampaging enemies are picked from the Elementalist pile, except for those on tiles placed during setup — those enemy tokens gain the Ambush ability. Whenever a rampaging Elementalist enemy token is defeated, the player who defeated it may add a new map tile as if they had explored (this new tile does not have to be adjacent to that player's Hero).
- **Exploring**: the valley is difficult to find and there is clearly sorcery at work — exploring costs triple the normal amount of Move. Any abilities that modify the Move cost of exploring apply *before* the tripling. Influence may be used as Move for the purpose of exploring.
- **Cities**: when the White City tile is placed, place the Hidden Valley location tile over the city space, with the Elementalist's enemy tokens face up per the faction leader's level. You must move into the Hidden Valley space to attack the High Priestess and her followers — it is not a safe space. The keep on the White City tile is defended by 2 green Elementalist enemy tokens instead of the normal gray enemy token. If attacked by a player after another player has already conquered it, a random gray enemy token is used as the defender instead, as usual.
- **Liberate Location**: none of the Hidden Valley's effects can be used until it has been liberated. Once liberated it acts as normal.
- **Additional special rules**: start with 1 Fame (instead of 0); +1 extra Fame whenever you cross a line on the Fame track. Start with +2 Reputation (a bonus of +1 for interaction, from the start). One extra die in the Source, and one extra Unit in the offer.

## Competitive

- Players: 2 to 4. Type: Competitive.
- Map Shape: Wedge (2 or 3 players), or Open limited to 4 columns (4 players).
- Countryside tiles: 5, 7, or 9.
- Core city tiles: 2 — the White City tile and the Green City tile (Green City is friendly: each player puts one Shield token on it, no-one is leader).
- Core non-city tiles: 2, 3, or 4 (equal to the number of players).
- Elementalist leader tile: represents the High Priestess, starting level = number of players + 3.

**Scenario end**: the High Priestess is defeated → all players get one last turn (game ends immediately if the Round ends during this).

**Scoring**: apply standard Achievements Scoring. In addition, count each player's Shield tokens on the Elementalist leader token — the most gets +5 Fame ("Greatest Enemy" of that faction). Ties broken in favor of whoever's Shield token sits on the highest level of the faction leader token.

## Cooperative

- Players: 2 to 4. Type: Cooperative.
- Map Shape: Fully Open.
- Countryside tiles: 6, 8, or 10.
- Core city tiles: 2, same as Competitive (White City, Green City friendly).
- Core non-city tiles: 2, 3, or 4 (equal to the number of players). Elementalist leader tile starts at level = 2 × number of players + 4.
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player.
- Cards/Skills: remove the four competitive Spells (109–112); use the Lost Legion expansion's cooperative interactive Skill tokens instead of competitive ones; remove competitive Skills from every player's Skill deck, including the Dummy Player's.
- Additional rules: Dummy Player takes a random Tactic first, then the real players choose theirs. Team Rules apply, except everyone is one team.

**Scenario end**: the High Priestess is defeated → all players (except the Dummy Player) get one last turn (game ends immediately if the Round ends during this).

**Scoring**: one team score. Base = the lowest Fame among all players. Apply standard Achievements Scoring, except each category only scores the single player with the highest value (most negative for Greatest Beating) — no Titles. Then:
- +20 points for defeating the High Priestess.
- +20 points if every player has at least one Shield token on the faction leader token.
- +30 points for each Round finished before the Round limit.
- +1 point for each card remaining in the Dummy Player's Deed deck not yet flipped this Round.
- +5 points if "End of the Round" was not yet announced this Round.

## Solo

- Players: 1. Type: Solo.
- Map Shape: Wedge.
- Countryside tiles: 5.
- Core city tiles: 2 — the White City tile and the Green City tile (both the player and the Dummy Player put a Shield token on it, no-one is leader).
- Core non-city tiles: 1. Elementalist leader tile starts at level 4.
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player.
- Cards/Skills: same as Cooperative (remove Spells 109–112; use Lost Legion cooperative interactive Skill tokens; remove competitive Skills from both decks).
- Additional rules: the player always chooses Tactics first, the Dummy Player takes one random card from those remaining. At the end of each Day/Night, remove both used Tactic cards from the game.

**Scenario end**: the High Priestess is defeated → the player has one last turn (the Dummy Player does not).

**Scoring**: base = your Fame. Apply standard Achievements Scoring, no Titles. Then:
- +20 points for defeating the High Priestess.
- +30 points for each Round finished before the Round limit.
- +1 point for each card remaining in the Dummy Player's Deed deck not yet flipped this Round.
- +5 points if "End of the Round" was not yet announced this Round.

## Outcome (Won/Lost) — all variants

Won iff the High Priestess was defeated; Lost otherwise. A score is always counted either way — the rulebook is explicit that failing still lets you "count your score to see how good you were."
