# First Reconnaissance

Source: `Mage-Knight-The-Apocalypse-Dragon---Rulebook-WEB.pdf`, pages 22–23.

A training scenario: locate and reveal the map's capital city before running out of Rounds. The competitive version of this scenario is in the base game rulebook (not extracted here); this expansion adds Cooperative and Solo variants for introducing new players to the game. Not a v1 target (v1 is Solo Conquest only) — captured here as reference for when this scenario gets implemented.

## Overview

- Purpose: training scenario — reveal the map's capital city.
- Cities may not be conquered or entered in this scenario.
- Gold Units are not used in this scenario.
- Spells and Advanced Action cards should not be revealed until needed for the first time.
- A player scores 1 Fame whenever they reveal a new tile (of any type).

## Setup

### Cooperative

- Players: 2 to 4.
- Length: Three rounds (2 days and 1 night).
- Map Shape: Wedge, no limitations.
- Countryside tiles: 8, 9, or 11 (for 2, 3, or 4 players), sorted by numbers.
- Core city tiles: 1. Core non-city tiles: 2.
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player.
- Cards/Skills: remove the four competitive Spells; remove the one competitive interactive Skill from each player's Skill tokens; use cooperative interactive Skill tokens if available.
- When taking Tactics, the Dummy Player takes a random Tactic first, then the players choose theirs. At the end of the first Day, agree and remove one of the Tactics used by the players (not the Dummy Player) from the game. Team Rules apply, except everyone is one team.

### Solo

- Players: 1.
- Length: Four rounds (2 days and 2 nights).
- Map Shape: Wedge, no limitations.
- Countryside tiles: 8, sorted by numbers.
- Core city tiles: 1. Core non-city tiles: 2.
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player.
- Cards/Skills: same removal/substitution as Cooperative.
- When taking Tactics, the player always chooses first; the Dummy Player then takes one random card from those remaining. At the end of each Day or Night, remove both used Tactic cards from the game.

## Scenario end

- Cooperative: when a player reveals the city, all players (including them) get one last turn. If the Round ends during this, the game ends immediately.
- Solo: when you reveal the city, you get one last turn (the Dummy Player does not).

## Scoring

### Cooperative

Success condition: the city is revealed (team-wide).

One team score: the lowest Fame of all players as the base score. Then apply standard Achievements scoring, except:
- In each category, only the player with the highest score (or most negative points for Greatest Beating) is counted.
- No Titles are awarded.

Then add, for the team:
- +10 points if the city was revealed.
- +30 points per Round finished before the Round limit.
- +1 point per card remaining in the Dummy Player's Deed deck (not yet flipped this Round).
- +5 points if "End of the Round" was not announced in the last Round.

### Solo

Success condition: the city is revealed.

Base = your Fame. Apply standard Achievements scoring, except no Titles are awarded. Then add:
- +10 points if the city was revealed.
- +30 points per Round finished before the Round limit.
- +1 point per card remaining in the Dummy Player's Deed deck (not yet flipped this Round).
- +5 points if "End of the Round" was not announced in the last Round.

## Outcome (Won/Lost)

- Won iff the city was revealed by the end of the game (team-wide for Cooperative); otherwise Lost.
- A score is always computed either way.
