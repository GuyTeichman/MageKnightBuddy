# The Lost Relic

Source: `Mage-Knight-Board-Game-Ultimate-Edition-Expansion-Rule-Books-September-2018.pdf` (Shades of Tezla expansion, Scenario Book), pages 27–28.

Not a v1 target (v1 is Solo Conquest only) — captured here as reference for when this scenario gets implemented.

## Overview

- Length: Two Rounds (1 day and 1 night).
- Purpose: find all pieces of an ancient relic, broken apart and scattered among the ruins of old cities.

### Special rules (apply to all variants)

- **Enemy tokens**: any rampaging draconum on core tiles are rampaging green enemies instead.
- **Cities**: the cities are destroyed — do not use city figures or city cards. They cost 2 Move to enter, and there is room for only 1 character per space. There is one piece of the relic in each destroyed city. When a destroyed city is discovered, place a draconum marker face down on the space. Whenever a player is adjacent to that space (day or night), reveal the marker — this is not rampaging, but entering the space fights the dragon; if successful, grab the relic piece and mark the space with your Shield token. One extra die in the Source, and one extra Unit in the offer.
- **Cards**: remove all Elite Units that can only be recruited in cities.
- **Additional special rules**: each player begins the game at level 3 using the "Starting at a Higher Level" variant rule (below) — this variant is required for this scenario. Start with 9 Fame; +1 extra Fame whenever you cross a line on the Fame track. A player scores 1 Fame whenever they reveal a new map tile, +1 more if the tile contains a ruined city.

### Variant rule: Starting at a Higher Level

Required for this scenario. Before setup (laying out the map, dealing the offers), each player creates their character:

- For each even-numbered level, each player draws 2 Skills, chooses one, and puts the other in the common Skill pool. So starting at level 2 or 3 does this once; level 4 or 5 does it twice; and so on.
- Start with the appropriate Command tokens for the starting level, and 2 crystals of the colors shown on the bottom of the character card.
- Each player takes, for free, one Advanced Action per odd-numbered level after the first (one at level 3, two at level 5, ...).
- Each player then has 6 Influence per level to spend buying cards, per this table:

  | Type of card | # to draw | Influence cost |
  |---|---|---|
  | Advanced Action | character level + 2 | 6 points per card beyond the free ones |
  | Regular Units | character level + 1 | the Unit's normal Influence cost |
  | Spells | character level | 9 points, or 7 points + a crystal of the matching color |
  | Artifacts | character level − 1 | 12 points |
  | Crystal | — | 3 points |

- Any Influence left over grants +1 Fame, no matter how much remains.
- Cards not selected are shuffled back into their decks; purchased Units are arranged as normal; purchased cards are shuffled into the player's starting Deed deck, then drawn up to the hand limit. Setup then proceeds as normal.
- Example: starting at level 3, a player has 18 Influence and draws 5 Advanced Actions, 4 Units, 3 Spells, 2 Artifacts. They take one Advanced Action for free, then spend the remaining Influence on Peasants (4), a Spell (9), and a crystal (3) — 16 of 18 spent, leaving 2 Influence → 1 Fame.
- Adjusting difficulty: the Influence-per-level rate, and the starting level itself, can both be tuned up or down.

## Competitive

- Players: 2 to 4. Type: Competitive.
- Map Shape: Open limited to 3 columns (2 players), Open limited to 4 columns (3 players), Fully Open (4 players).
- Countryside tiles: 6, 7, or 8.
- Core city tiles: 2, 3, or 4 (equal to the number of players).
- Core non-city tiles: 1, 2, or 3 (one less than the number of players).

**Scenario end**: all parts of the relic have been collected → all players (including the one who found the last part) get one more turn (game ends immediately if the Round ends during this).

**Scoring**: apply standard Achievements Scoring. In addition, players score 4 Fame for each piece of the relic they collected. Whoever scores the most Fame from this gets an additional +4 Fame as the Greatest Relic Hunter (+2 Fame if tied). If all parts of the relic were found, the mission was successful — win/lose or not, whoever has the most Fame wins the game.

## Cooperative

- Players: 2 to 4 (playing with 4 requires the Lost Legion expansion). Type: Cooperative.
- Map Shape: Open limited to 4 columns (2 players), Fully Open (3 or 4 players).
- Countryside tiles: 7, 8, or 9.
- Core city tiles: 3, 4, or 5 (one more than the number of players). If playing 4 players, use Volkare's starting tile as a 5th destroyed city.
- Core non-city tiles: 2, 3, or 4 (equal to the number of players).
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player.
- Cards/Skills: remove the four competitive Spells (109–112); use the Lost Legion expansion's cooperative interactive Skill tokens instead of competitive ones; remove competitive Skills from every player's Skill deck, including the Dummy Player's.
- Additional rules: Team Rules apply, except everyone is one team. Dummy Player takes a random Tactic first, then the real players choose theirs.

**Scenario end**: all parts of the relic have been collected → all players (except the Dummy Player) get one more turn (game ends immediately if the Round ends during this).

**Scoring**: one team score. Base = the lowest Fame among all players. Apply standard Achievements Scoring, except each category only scores the single player with the highest value (most negative for Greatest Beating) — no Titles. Then:
- +5 points for each piece of the relic found.
- +5 points if every player found at least 1 piece of the relic.
- +10 points if all pieces of the relic were found.
- +1 point for each card remaining in the Dummy Player's Deed deck not yet flipped this Round.
- +5 points if "End of the Round" was not yet announced this Round.

## Solo

- Players: 1. Type: Solo.
- Map Shape: Open limited to 3 columns.
- Countryside tiles: 6.
- Core city tiles: 2. Core non-city tiles: 1.
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player.
- Cards/Skills: same as Cooperative (remove Spells 109–112; use Lost Legion cooperative interactive Skill tokens; remove competitive Skills from both decks).
- Additional rules: the player always chooses Tactics first, the Dummy Player takes one random card from those remaining. At the end of each Day/Night, remove both used Tactic cards from the game.

**Scenario end**: all parts of the relic have been collected → the player has one last turn (the Dummy Player does not).

**Scoring**: base = your Fame. Apply standard Achievements Scoring, no Titles. Then:
- +5 points for each piece of the relic found.
- +10 points if all pieces of the relic were found.
- +1 point for each card remaining in the Dummy Player's Deed deck not yet flipped this Round.
- +5 points if "End of the Round" was not yet announced this Round.

## Outcome (Won/Lost) — Cooperative and Solo

Won iff all parts of the relic were collected; Lost otherwise. A score is always counted either way. (Competitive has no binary win/loss — see its Scoring above: whoever has the most Fame wins regardless of whether all relic pieces were found.)
