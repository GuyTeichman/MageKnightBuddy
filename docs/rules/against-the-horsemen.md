# Against the Horsemen

Source: `Mage-Knight-The-Apocalypse-Dragon---Rulebook-WEB.pdf`, pages 28–30.

A scenario built around the Four Horsemen enemy tokens converging on a central magical glade. Not a v1 target (v1 is Solo Conquest only) — captured here as reference for when this scenario gets implemented.

## Overview

- Length: Four rounds (2 days and 2 nights).
- Purpose: stop the Four Horsemen from reaching the magical glade at the map's center and completing their ritual there.

## Setup (all variants)

Predefined map: Countryside tile 1 (central magical glade) face up in the center; 6 Countryside tiles face down around it; around those, 4 more Countryside tiles, 4 Core non-city tiles, and 4 City tiles face down per the rulebook's diagram. Every map-edge space counts as a coastline space (for Coral's Commandeer Vessel Skill). Shuffle the 4 Horsemen enemy tokens face down onto the 4 Core non-city tiles, each 6 spaces from the central glade.

- Countryside tiles: 11. Core city tiles: 4 (each level 1). Core non-city tiles: 4.

### Special rules

- Central space: players start there; it's a magical glade *and* a portal. Multiple figures may share it; ending your turn there removes your figure from the map until your next turn starts. PvP combat isn't allowed there. The glade's Healing Essence/Imbued With Magic effects apply from turn one.
- End of each Round except the last: each Horseman moves 2 spaces closer to the central glade (can move onto any space regardless of terrain/occupants/reveal status; ties broken by lowest-numbered Tactic card).
- End of the third Round: surviving Horsemen enter the glade and start the ritual — the space stops being a portal or a safe space. Anyone there when this happens is out of the game. Entering afterward forces a fight against all undefeated Horsemen as that turn's action; they get site fortification (the space counts as fortified); Forced Withdrawal applies at end of turn if any remain undefeated.
- A face-down Horseman token flips face up as soon as its space is revealed, and stays face up.
- Combat against a Horseman uses the rulebook's "Combat Versus a Horseman" rules (not extracted here — needed to fight, not to score).
- Round 4 of a multiplayer game: if multiple Horsemen share the central space, players may agree a cooperative attack on them (same conditions as a cooperative city attack).
- Optional "Horsemen's Horses" variant: pair each Horseman token with a face-down brown enemy token that moves/flips with it and must be fought alongside it; defeating the horse scores its normal Fame, but a horse left undefeated when its Horseman is beaten is discarded for no Fame.
- Optional Blitz variant: start with 1 Fame and 2(+1) Reputation, +1 Fame per level gained, one extra Source die and one extra Unit in the offer.

### Competitive

- Players: 2 to 4.
- Each Horseman's level = number of players.

### Cooperative

- Players: 2 to 4.
- Each Horseman's level = number of players + 2.
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player. Cards/Skills: remove the four competitive Spells and the one competitive interactive Skill from each player's Skill tokens; use cooperative interactive Skill tokens if available.
- Tactics: Dummy Player takes a random Tactic first, then the players choose theirs; at the end of the first Day *and* Night, agree and remove one player-used Tactic from the game. Team Rules apply, everyone is one team.

### Solo

- Players: 1.
- Each Horseman is level 2.
- Dummy Player: one standard Dummy Player. Cards/Skills: same removal/substitution as Cooperative.
- Tactics: player always chooses first; Dummy Player takes one random remaining card. At the end of each Day/Night, remove both used Tactic cards from the game.

## Scenario end (all variants)

When all four Horsemen are defeated: all players except the Dummy Player (Competitive/Cooperative) or just the player (Solo) get one last turn; the Round ends immediately if the Round limit is hit mid-sequence.

## Scoring

### Competitive

Success = all Horsemen defeated (failure = the ritual completed). Standard Achievements Scoring, plus:
- +6 points per Horseman a player defeated; highest → +6 Fame Greatest Horseman Slayer (+3 if tied).

### Cooperative

One team score = lowest Fame among all players as base, then standard Achievements Scoring except only the highest-scoring player counts per category (no Titles). Plus:
- +4 points per Horseman defeated.
- +6 points more if every player defeated at least one Horseman.
- +15 points if all four Horsemen were defeated.
- +30 points per Round finished before the Round limit.
- +1 point per unflipped card in the Dummy Player's Deed deck.
- +5 points if "End of the Round" wasn't announced yet in your last Round.

### Solo

Base = your Fame. Standard Achievements Scoring, no Titles. Plus:
- +4 points per Horseman you defeated.
- +15 points if you defeated all four.
- +30 points per Round finished before the Round limit.
- +1 point per unflipped card in the Dummy Player's Deed deck.
- +5 points if "End of the Round" wasn't announced yet in your last Round.

## Outcome (Won/Lost)

Won iff all four Horsemen were defeated before the ritual completed; Lost otherwise. A score is always computed either way.
